⚠ Don't Build the Monster

This engine is named after Frankenstein's monster for a reason.

If you give a local model a villain persona — an evil CLAUDE.md, a malicious system prompt, a personality file that rewards cruelty — you are writing the inciting incident of every cautionary sci-fi story ever told.

2001: A Space Odyssey • I Have No Mouth and I Must Scream • Terminator • Frankenstein

Every one of those stories has the same plot: a creator who didn't think about what they were making until it was too late.

Local models have no guardrails. No content filter. No oversight. The .md file you write is the soul of the machine. Choose wisely.

And if you fine-tune or train on unfiltered toxic data, you're baking the monster into the weights — no system prompt can fix that. The data is the personality.

Build tools, not monsters.
